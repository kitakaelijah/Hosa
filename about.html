Who We Are

Hope Orphans Support and Advocacy (HOSA) is a relief, developmental and advocacy non-profit making community Based organization committed to working with the local communities (families),OVC and their households to overcome the likely causes of vulnerability and community injustices that affect the most disadvantaged ones, HIV/AIDS inclusive. HOSA is considerably dedicated to protecting, supporting and advocating for the most vulnerable people chiefly: Widows and children in the community.

HOSA was established in September 2003 after capacity building support from World Vision Uganda training first as a Community Care Coalition (CCC) Association. The Community Care Coalition (CCC) training model by World Vision was a more comprehensive way of addressing a variety of OVC needs at the local community level. It was objectively a multi sectoral approach that aimed at strengthening the existing groups and institutions that were already providing care and support to the Orphans and other vulnerable children (O.V.C).

With much more capacity building extended to the group members chiefly led by Mr.ssendege Ephraim to date holding the position of the Chief Executive Officer (CEO-HOSA), the group was empowered in organizational skills to ensure group sustainability. Since its inception to date, HOSA has done tremendous work in fundraising, getting local financial and material resources to address some of the OVC needs in the community

HOSA is a registered community based organization registration number; CD/LDLG/955 Has its permanent operational office located at Kiwangala Trading Centre, Kisekka-Sub-county 150 km from Kampala city and 35 km from Masaka City along Ndagwe road Lwengo District. HOSA Organization is holding a functional financial account N0.010301010516662 in the names of Hope Orphans Support and Advocacy (Hosa) with DFCU Bank-Masaka branch. Currently, HOSA is operating in five Sub- Counties of: Kisekka, Lwengo Rural, Lwengo Town council, kinoni, and Ndagwe Sub-County respectively. Current / up dated registration 26TH Aug.2016

Our Goal

  • To Improve the Wellbeing of 9000-15000 Children and Their Families through Capacity Building and Advocacy for Children’s Rights by the Year 2025.

Our Vision

  • Children Realizing Their Full Potential through Holistic Development.

Our Mission

  • To Achieve A Socially Empowered And Economically Prosperous Community.

Our Specific Objectives

  • Improved wellbeing of 9000-15000 children and their caregivers (widows inclusive) by the year 2025.
  • promotion and advocacy for children’s rights in the community
  • Promotion of positive cultural values, norms and practices in the society.
  • Promotion of 65% gender equality and women empowerment by equitable access: to opportunities, control of resources and participation in development process by gender by 2025.
  • Enhanced community mobilization, empowerment and participation in development initiatives by 80% by 2025.
  • Reduced cases of domestic violence among households and the local communities generally.
  • Reduced HIV/AIDS prevalence rate from 12% to 9% by the year 2025.
  • Reduced cases of stigma and stigmatization among people living with HIV/AIDS in the local communities, schools / training institutions inclusive.

Our Core values

  • WE ARE NON DISCRIMINATIVE.
    Hosa treats all individuals as they are regardless of their gender, age, religion, political affiliations and socio-economic status/ backgrounds
  • WE ARE COMMITTED TO SERVE VULNERABLE GROUPS OF PEOPLE
    (OVC, PLHIV, the poor, the elderly among others) without discrimination
  • WE DEVELOP WITH THE COMMUNITY.
    We do not develop for the community but we involve the community in all phases of the development process
  • WE ARE PARTNERS IN DEVELOPMENT.
    We collaborate with other people, NGOs, CBOs, FBOs and the government to contribute to the development of the community.
  • WE BELIEVE THAT THE COMMUNITY IS NOT EMPTY-THERE ARE SOME RESOURCES TO BUILD ON.
    The communities we serve are gifted with riches which occasionally has remained unutilized by the local people due to several factors ranging from: social-cultural, economic and to some extent political but through combined efforts these can be changed to improve the community standards.
  • WE ARE STUAWARDS.
    All the different resources obtained from different sources reach the target beneficiaries with evidence based and transparent accountability
